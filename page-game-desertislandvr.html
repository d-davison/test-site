Desert Island VR

Info

VR Game - 2021

This project explorers how VR can be used as a tool for teaching patients of chronic pain self management techniques.

The project is a product where research is conducted to further an understand of a certain area to help design a product to help in a certain scenario. For this project, I chose to look into Chronic Pain, what is is, and how I can possibly use VR as a tool to help combat the problem. With my research I found that the self-management of the condition was the most effective and cost efficient treatment, and with this information have started to design and develop a prototype for people who are suffering for Chronic Pain

The game has the user take part in four different activities around the island before evaluating themselves and looking at how they have done, and how they have progressed. The tasks are designed to stretch out the players arms and neck, and to encourage the player to problem solve, all while moving around.

This project was developed as part of my Final Year Project at the University of Greenwich. I recieved a first (71%) for this piece.

Overview:

The game includes several main mechanics and features:

  • Progress Tracker to track and show how the user might be improving with the exersizes
  • 4 Main Activites
  • Game customisation including Night or Day mode and Easy/Medium/Hard
  • Finished In April 2021
